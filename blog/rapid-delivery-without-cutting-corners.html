Rapid delivery without cutting corners

How we ship MVPs and web apps in weeks while keeping quality high and tech debt low.

Fast delivery and quality are not opposites. Speed fails when scope is fuzzy or the stack is experimental. Speed succeeds with tight scope, proven technology and visible progress.

Stack choices that respect your timeline

We use proven stacks: modern React frontends, Node or Python APIs, Postgres, managed hosting. The goal is maintainability for your team after handover, not resume-driven framework choices.

Scope is the main quality lever

The biggest risk to quality is scope creep. New ideas go to a backlog unless they are essential to the agreed outcome. That discipline protects auth, billing and data model from last-minute patches.

What we refuse to rush

  • Authentication and authorisation checks.
  • Payment webhooks and idempotency.
  • Database migrations and backups.
  • Secrets management and HTTPS everywhere.

What we accept in v1

See technical debt on SaaS MVPs: duplicated UI is fine; ambiguous data ownership is not.

How you stay in control

Short cycles, staging demos and written change log. You steer while the work is fresh, not after a month in the dark.

Recent delivery examples

Live SaaS in 21 days (OhMyPod). Forex research platform in 12 days. Lead magnet tool in 9 days. Different domains, same scoping discipline.
